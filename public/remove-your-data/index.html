The Invisible Market: Your Data

Data brokers are companies you've likely never heard of, yet they collect, compile, and sell your personal information to anyone willing to pay. This includes your address, phone number, family members, purchasing habits, and even political affiliations.

What is a Data Broker?

A data broker is a business that collects personal information about consumers and sells that information to other organizations. They often gather data from public records, social media, and other online sources, then combine it to create detailed profiles of individuals.

Real Examples of Data Brokerage

Sites like Whitepages, BeenVerified, and Spokeo aggregate vast amounts of personal data, making it publicly accessible. This data can be used for targeted advertising, identity theft, or even harassment.

Why Should You Care?

Your data is valuable. When it's in the hands of data brokers, it's out of your control. This can lead to increased spam calls, phishing attempts, and a significant risk to your personal security and privacy.
