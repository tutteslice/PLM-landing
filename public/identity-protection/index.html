The Growing Threat of Identity Theft

Identity theft is a serious and growing problem, with millions of people affected each year. Protecting your personal information online is more critical than ever.

What is Identity Theft?

Identity theft occurs when someone uses your personal identifying information, like your name, Social Security number, or credit card number, without your permission, to commit fraud or other crimes. This can include opening new credit accounts, filing fraudulent tax returns, or even getting medical care in your name.

How Does Identity Theft Happen?

Data Breaches

Large-scale data breaches at companies, governments, or healthcare providers can expose millions of records, including names, addresses, Social Security numbers, and more.

Phishing Scams

Criminals use deceptive emails, texts, or websites to trick you into revealing personal information. These can look legitimate but are designed to steal your data.

Malware and Viruses

Malicious software installed on your devices can secretly collect your personal data, including login credentials, banking information, and other sensitive details.

Public Wi-Fi Vulnerabilities

Unsecured public Wi-Fi networks can be hotspots for cybercriminals to intercept your data as it travels across the network.
