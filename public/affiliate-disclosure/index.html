What is an Affiliate Disclosure?

An affiliate disclosure is a statement that informs you, the reader, that we have a financial relationship with some of the companies or products mentioned on our website. This means that if you click on certain links and make a purchase, we may receive a commission at no extra cost to you.

How Do Affiliate Links Work?

When you click on an affiliate link on Private Lives Matter, a special tracking cookie is placed on your browser. If you then make a purchase on the linked website, the cookie helps the merchant track that you came from our site, and we earn a small percentage of the sale.

Our Commitment to You

While we do earn commissions from affiliate links, our primary goal is to provide valuable and unbiased information to help you protect your privacy. We only recommend products and services that we have researched thoroughly and genuinely believe will benefit our audience. Our editorial content is not influenced by affiliate partnerships.

Why Do We Use Affiliate Links?

Running a website and producing high-quality, in-depth content requires resources. Affiliate commissions help us cover the costs of maintaining Private Lives Matter, including hosting, research, content creation, and technical support. This allows us to keep our core content free for everyone.

Your Support

By using our affiliate links, you are directly supporting Private Lives Matter and enabling us to continue our mission of advocating for digital rights and privacy. We greatly appreciate your support!
